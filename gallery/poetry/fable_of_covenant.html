    As told by Storykeeper Comet

    Part 1: The Survivor

    To tell you the story of the Traveler, I must tell you a different story first.

    Once, there was a Survivor.
    It was one of the very last, a living reminder that the Gods were not all-powerful.

    Indeed, the Gods were not even always present.
    The Survivor remembered a time before them, when its people were mighty.
    When the Gods began to move against them, they stood strong as a Braid of Braids.

    But the Gods were heinous and cunning.
    The Survivor's people, of the Materia as they were, held many things dear to them.
    Chief among them were those Eternal Elders, those of them that had seen more than any other.

    The Gods' assault on the cities broke easily under the sheer Materia the Survivor's people weilded.
    The Survivor's people sent their armies to give chase, seeking utter ruin upon the Gods that wanted
    them eradicated.
    They realized all too late, however, that the siege was nothing more than a distraction.

    They returned from war to find their homes in ruin, the Gods' trap laid bare:
    In their haste to bring utter destruction to the Gods' worshipers, they had left themselves
    defenseless.
    They found their walls shattered, their cities razed, and, worst of all, their precious Elders had
    been taken.

    The Survivor's people - or what was left of them - scattered to the winds, fleeing the persecution
    of the Gods.
    The Gods, ageless as they were, would never stop hunting them.

    Part 2: The Traveller

    Once, there was a Traveler.
    He lived his life in solitude, traveling through the Godless lands as he did.

    He was not always a Traveler.
    His greatest gift, his mind, was also his greatest curse, for it was his very thoughts that had
    cursed him to wander.
    He had no great wants or dreams, save for this: he wanted to live.
    And the Godless lands would not harbor that dream.

    The Traveler wandered for many years, searching for refuge.
    He did not find it often.

    Once, there was a Survivor.
    It had seen the collapse of its people, and carried the weight of their stories on its back.
    It sought revenge.

    The Traveler woke in a forest that had not been there the night before.
    He had seen many things in his time in the Godless lands, but this was new.

    His wanderings brought him to the center of the forest, where he found a grand oak, golden light
    pulsing softly under its bark.
    This was someplace sacred, and older than the gods, he recognized.
    He felt the earth beneath his feet breathe as he did.
    He set down his meager belongings, and went to make camp.

    The Traveler met the Survivor in a place of great heartbreak, though this was not known to the
    Traveler.
    Standing where the Gods had stolen the last of its peoples' magic, the Survivor knew this to be
    true:
    History does not repeat itself, but it often rhymes.

    The Survivor asked the Traveler for nothing more than deliverance.
    It offered no great power to move upon the world, other than this:
    The Traveler would stand against the Gods themselves and be unfaltering still.
    He would not have the strength of a thousand armies, save for the people that would follow him.
    The Survivor asked for deliverance, and the Traveler sought revenge.
    And so the Covenant was struck, and so began the era so named.

    Part 3: The Covenant

    A Covenant of two moved upon the world.
    The purpose the Survivor had granted him had changed the Traveler.
    He no longer wandered through the Godless lands, but rather marched.

    The first God they marched upon was one of Purity.

    The Traveler found himself before the Shining City.
    A magnum opus. A cold tower of steel, rising high above the sun-scorched ground.
    His arrival needed no grand introduction, for the Gods could feel his footsteps shake the world.

    The gates opened wide before him.
    He was expected.

    The God of Purity called the Traveler to his palace.
    They asked why he had come.
    He had come, the Traveler explained, to offer a choice:
    Lay down their magic, or die by his hand.

    The God of Purity laughed.
    The Traveler did not.
    The God of Purity lay cracked and broken on the pristine white floor.

    The Traveler stepped out to find the Shining City in chaos.
    The rain beat down over his head in sheets.
    Stepping through a cracked shell of a prison, he saw a frail figure, completely untouched by the
    rain.
    The Traveler saw the Castaway, there in the broken shell of the Shining City.
    He had found the second member of his Covenant.

    The Covenant marched, and as it marched, it grew.
    Its members were as varied as the Gods they sought.

    Gods of Water, of Magic, of the Sand spread on the beaches of Gaia.
    Their truth was that of Change.
    The Gods that chose Change and kindness gave the world this:
    Magic would never again be chained.
    They could not bring back the Survivor's people, but they could ensure that the remnants would never
    again want for revenge.

    But there were others, too.
    Gods of War, of Steel, of the Sinew hiding beneath the skin of every living thing.
    Their truth was the Ashes.
    The Traveler felt their magic culminating, making their final curse, and he gave the world this:
    The Gods would never again harm his Covenant.

    With one great stroke of his blade, he split the materia down the middle, stopping the curse in its
    tracks.
    He knew exactly what it would cost him.
    The Traveler laid down his life to spare ours from the Gods.
    His final ask was as simple as he was in life:
    Do not worship him.
    He is exactly where he wants to be.

    • we fight because we like it

    (written: apr 7, 2025)

    it is quiet here.
    this space, not empty, but
    full with a tense silence.

    the people here are like me.
    contradictory living outside the lines.
    the silence whispers to us that *things can be different.*

    and the daughter whose parents still call her a son,
    the two men, desperately in love, who are forced to live apart,
    the immigrant who arrived here to chains and tyranny,
    the woman who fights on for memory of her family,
    the weird kid who feels more kinship with animals than people,
    the refugee, fleeing bombs and death in their home,
    the boy who is only still here because of his friends,
    the soldier who put down their weapons when they saw who they were hurting,
    the metalworker who strikes because they cannot kill the steel barons,
    the man finally out of prison after twenty years,
    the boy who cannot stand being limited,
    the girl who cannot just stand by while she watches her friends die,

    i think we all know.
    *things can be different,* but only if we make them different.
    some of us fight because we have to, but mostly,
    we fight because we like it.
